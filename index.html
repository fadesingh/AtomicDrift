Maps Of Latent Space

In many ancient & medieval cultures, the idea of a standardised minimal education for an elite member of society was considered important. Whether it was the quadrivium in Europe, Paideia in Greece, the Four Arts of China, vedanga in India, Edubba in Mesopotamia…the curricula were responses to the state of the world at that time.

The skills of the Chinese scholar-gentleman are particularly interesting because of their specific priorities. They required mastery of a stringed musical instrument (guqin), the board game Go, calligraphy, and painting. The Six Arts learned by the 72 famous disciples of Confucius included archery, equestrianism, and most notably - mathematics .

What kind of knowledge is essential to know in the year 2025?

Most people—even smart, educated people—are completely unprepared for what's coming. What we now call AI is mathematical physics (that is where the word “model” comes from) applied to information. Moreover, we are living in a highly uncertain world, and therefore we need to understand the science of uncertainty and stochasticity - randomness.

This isn't about learning how to code. It's about grasping why your coffee cup is actually a probability cloud. It's about seeing the mathematical scaffolding that holds up everything from market dynamics to cosmic evolution. In 2025, that's not theoretical knowledge—it's survival gear.

The Course

This crash course takes the idea of a point particle ( an “atom”) and places it in different contexts with each successive module. Through these imaginary models we explore fundamental ideas like waves and rhythms in nature (the simple harmonic oscillator), randomness in the art of Jackson Pollock, building up to more complex phenomenon like earthquakes inside stars and the seizures of financial markets (time series analysis, Markov chains). Along the way you will gain a footing in calculus and linear algebra, two of the most powerful tools for mathematical modelling of anything.
